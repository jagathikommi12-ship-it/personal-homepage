About

The short version: I take things apart to understand them, then build something better.

Bio Experience Leadership & Volunteer Education Coursework Awards Skills

I'm a Computer Science undergrad at UMass Amherst (Business minor, for when the venture-capital instincts kick in), originally from Hyderabad, India. I didn't get into CS for the elegance of recursion — I got into it because I wanted to build things people would actually use, not just things that compile.

These days that means full-stack apps with AI quietly doing the heavy lifting: an HR system that predicts who's about to quit before they do, a proctored exam platform a Fortune 500 company trusted with real candidates, and a job tracker that coaches you through your own job hunt better than your group chat does.

Outside of that, I'm probably teaching someone to read — I've spent the last few years tutoring English through Read A Story, which taught me more about patience and explaining things clearly than any data structures class did. Turns out "wait, can you explain that differently?" is a skill that transfers directly to debugging.
